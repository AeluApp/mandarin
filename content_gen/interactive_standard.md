# Aelu Interactive Layer Writing Standard

**Rule:** Every interaction should feel like it belongs to a living world, while the correctness logic remains clear enough to learn from.

**Pedagogical constraint:** Do NOT design answer sets where the only difference between right and wrong is a subtle social nuance the learner has not yet been taught how to detect.

## Leveling

**HSK 1-3:** Right/wrong distinctions mostly objective. Factual correctness, sequence, who did what, obvious emotional cues. No delicate subtext.

**HSK 4-6:** Moderate nuance, teachable logic. Basic register, clear emotional responsiveness, simple face-saving, answering the real concern. The reason must be explainable.

**HSK 7-9:** More social/psychological nuance. But the best answer must be defensible with evidence. Wrong answers fail for identifiable reasons.

## Failure Modes to Eliminate

1. **Perfect Player** — correct answers that are polished mini-essays, too self-aware, too emotionally articulate. Should sound like something a smart human might say aloud.

2. **Cartoonish Wrong Answers** — absurd, irrelevant, stupid in a way no real learner would choose. Wrong answers should be wrong for human reasons.

3. **Generic Feedback** — no "Great job," "Thoughtful answer." Feedback should be in-scene reactions that reveal character.

4. **Hidden-Basis Correctness** — scoring depends on nuance the learner can't fairly detect. If they'd need native instinct, redesign.

## Correctness Bases (at least one must apply)

1. Factual fit (matches what was said/happened)
2. Pragmatic fit (addresses the actual question/problem)
3. Emotional fit (responds to text-supported cues)
4. Register fit (appropriate formality for setting)
5. Logic/inference fit (follows from evidence)

## Wrong Answer Types (plausibly attractive)

- factually wrong
- unsupported inference
- answers wrong part of question
- ignores visible emotional cue
- register mismatch
- too blunt / too distant / too formal
- false certainty
- slight misread of relationship
- self-protective dodge
- textbook-correct but socially odd

## Premium Feel Test

1. Best answer sounds like a human, not a model student?
2. Wrong answers wrong in believable ways?
3. Correctness basis teachable at this level?
4. Learner can explain why right answer is right?
5. NPC reaction reveals character, not just correctness?
6. Tests understanding, not mind-reading?
7. Mandarin natural for speech at this level?
8. Increases trust in the app's taste and intelligence?
