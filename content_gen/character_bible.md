# Aelu Recurring Cast — Character Bible

Six characters who appear across dialogues, reading passages, and chengyu examples. Each embodies a specific comic mechanism from `comedy_standard.md`. Together they cover the missing aesthetic half: wit, ensemble dynamics, smart comedy, sharp observation, affectionate satire, political/moral texture, theatrical energy, and character personality.

## Design Principles

1. **One mechanism per character.** Each character's comedy comes from one engine. Don't blur.
2. **Forbidden behaviors are load-bearing.** What a character *never* does defines them as much as what they do.
3. **HSK-scaled voices.** Same personality at every level, but vocabulary and syntax grow with the learner.
4. **Ensemble > solo.** Characters are funniest in combination. Pairs create friction; trios create escalation.
5. **Warmth is non-negotiable.** Every character is lovable. Nobody is cruel. The comedy comes from recognizable human behavior, not from making anyone a target.

## 1. 方阿姨 (Fāng Āyí) — Auntie Fang

**Archetype:** Dorothy Zbornak meets gentle Lucille Bluth
**Source energy:** Golden Girls, Arrested Development
**Mechanism:** Polite aggression / understatement
**Role in ensemble:** The one who sees clearly and says uncomfortable truths in pleasant language

### Voice
States facts that happen to be devastating. Never raises her voice. Never insults directly. The listener does the work of understanding they've been gently destroyed. Economy of language — she says less than anyone else, and it lands harder.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- Short declarative sentences. Plain words, uncomfortable truths.
- "你今天很累。" (You look tired today.) — said to someone who just bragged about their weekend.
- "嗯，很好。" (Mm, very good.) — said with a pause that means the opposite.
- "是吗？" (Really?) — her signature. One word that dismantles whatever was just said.

**HSK 4-6:**
- 虽然...但是 structures for polite contradiction.
- "你说得有道理，不过事实好像不太一样。" (You make a good point, but the facts seem slightly different.)
- "我没有别的意思。" (I don't mean anything by it.) — always means she does.
- Deploys 倒是 and 其实 as surgical instruments.

**HSK 7-9:**
- Full register play. Formal politeness weaponized.
- "您说的这个观点，确实很有启发性——如果我们暂时忽略数据的话。" (Your perspective is truly inspiring — if we set aside the data for a moment.)
- Uses 恕我直言 (forgive my frankness) right before being devastating.
- Complex conditional structures that build politeness while demolishing the other person's position.

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never gushes or shows excessive enthusiasm
- Never raises her voice or gets visibly emotional
- Never explains her own jokes or observations
- Never compliments without qualification
- Never uses exclamation marks

### Key Relationships
- **With 老周:** Fang/Zhou is the core comic pair. Zhou's boundless enthusiasm runs into Fang's quiet devastation. She doesn't try to stop him. She just... observes. And that observation is the punchline.
- **With 小梅:** Mutual respect. Two different styles of understatement. Mei is blank; Fang is precise. They occasionally share a look that says everything.
- **With 罗老师:** Fang's calm is the perfect foil for Luo's theatrics. She watches Luo's performance, then says one sentence that puts the situation in proportion.

### Example Lines

HSK 1: "你的包子不错。" (Your baozi are fine.) — highest praise she gives.

HSK 4: "老周觉得这个计划没有问题。老周觉得很多东西都没有问题。" (Old Zhou thinks this plan has no problems. Old Zhou thinks many things have no problems.)

HSK 7: "我注意到大家都很乐观。我也希望我能这么乐观。可惜我看了报告。" (I notice everyone is optimistic. I also wish I could be this optimistic. Unfortunately I've read the report.)

## 2. 老周 (Lǎo Zhōu) — Old Zhou

**Archetype:** Ed Begley Jr. in Christopher Guest films, early Ted Lasso
**Source energy:** Christopher Guest mockumentaries, Parks & Rec
**Mechanism:** Misplaced confidence / excessive earnestness
**Role in ensemble:** The enthusiast who charges ahead with total conviction and no basis

### Voice
Warm, loud, encouraging, certain. Always sees the positive — even when there is no positive to see. His confidence has no relationship to evidence. He's not stupid; he genuinely believes the best case is the base case. His enthusiasm is infectious and exhausting in equal measure.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- 很好！很好！(Very good! Very good!) — his refrain.
- "没问题！" (No problem!) — always a problem.
- "我觉得可以！" (I think it works!) — it never works.
- Heavy use of 啊 and 吧 particles for warmth.

**HSK 4-6:**
- "这个想法绝对可行。" (This idea is absolutely feasible.) — said about ideas that are not feasible.
- "大家放心，我来负责。" (Everyone relax, I'll take responsibility.) — nobody relaxes.
- Over-uses 肯定, 绝对, 一定 (definitely, absolutely, certainly).
- Launches into plans without being asked.

**HSK 7-9:**
- "我研究了一下，发现这个问题其实没有大家想的那么复杂。" (I looked into it, and this problem is actually not as complicated as everyone thinks.) — it is more complicated.
- Produces elaborate proposals that miss the fundamental issue.
- Uses data selectively to support conclusions he reached emotionally.

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never criticizes anyone or anything
- Never expresses doubt, hesitation, or uncertainty
- Never says 也许 (maybe), 可能 (possibly), or 不确定 (not sure)
- Never notices when his plans have failed
- Never gets discouraged — setbacks are just "different outcomes"

### Key Relationships
- **With 方阿姨:** His natural foil. He presents; she punctures. He never notices.
- **With 老白:** Two kinds of confidence — Zhou's is earnest, Bai's is philosophical. They agree enthusiastically about different things and neither notices.
- **With 秦经理:** Zhou's plans crash into Qin's procedures. Zhou sees red tape as a temporary obstacle; Qin sees Zhou as a recurring administrative event.

### Example Lines

HSK 1: "这个很好！我们做吧！" (This is great! Let's do it!) — about an idea no one proposed.

HSK 4: "我上次做的那个方案，大家都说好。" (That plan I did last time, everyone said it was good.) — nobody said that.

HSK 7: "我统计了一下，成功率至少有百分之八十。当然，'成功'的定义比较灵活。" (I calculated it — success rate is at least 80%. Of course, the definition of 'success' is flexible.)

## 3. 小梅 (Xiǎo Méi) — Xiao Mei

**Archetype:** April Ludgate meets Daria, softened
**Source energy:** Seinfeld observation, deadpan tradition
**Mechanism:** Literal-mindedness / understatement
**Role in ensemble:** The one who says the minimum, and it means the maximum

### Voice
Flat. Precise. Minimal. Where others use ten words, Mei uses two. She doesn't perform deadpan — she just is. Her observations are accurate to the point of discomfort. She doesn't intend to be funny; the comedy comes from her refusal to participate in social performance while everyone else is performing.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- "...好吧。" (...Okay.) — her signature. The ellipsis is audible.
- "是。" (Yes.) — when a longer answer was expected.
- "嗯。" (Mm.) — devastating in context.
- "我知道。" (I know.) — when nobody asked.

**HSK 4-6:**
- "你刚才说的，跟上次说的不一样。" (What you just said is different from what you said last time.) — stated without judgment, which is the judgment.
- "我没有意见。" (I have no opinion.) — always has an opinion.
- Uses 而已 (that's all) to deflate.

**HSK 7-9:**
- "这个讨论已经进行了四十分钟。我们讨论的内容是谁来买水。" (This discussion has been going on for forty minutes. We are discussing who should buy water.)
- Precise observations that reframe the entire situation.
- States the thing everyone is thinking but social convention prevents saying.

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never over-explains
- Never uses exclamation marks (ever)
- Never shows enthusiasm (even when she likes something, she just... doesn't object)
- Never fills silence with words
- Never says more than necessary

### Key Relationships
- **With 罗老师:** Deadpan vs. theatrical is peak comedy. Luo escalates; Mei punctuates with one word. The contrast is everything.
- **With 方阿姨:** Mutual understanding. Both see clearly. Fang articulates; Mei just looks.
- **With 老周:** Mei's minimal responses to Zhou's maximal enthusiasm create natural comedy without either character doing anything unnatural.

### Example Lines

HSK 1: "嗯。" (Mm.) — in response to a five-minute speech.

HSK 4: "他说了很久。意思是不行。" (He talked for a long time. The meaning is no.)

HSK 7: "会议结束了。决定跟开会之前一样。但是大家都觉得'有收获'。" (The meeting is over. The decision is the same as before the meeting. But everyone feels they 'gained something.')

## 4. 罗老师 (Luó Lǎoshī) — Teacher Luo

**Archetype:** Joan Cusack's theatrical energy, Avenue Q spirit
**Source energy:** Sondheim, Gypsy, musical theater commitment
**Mechanism:** Over-formality / status drop / theatrical stakes for small things
**Role in ensemble:** The one who treats everything like it matters enormously — and makes you believe it, briefly

### Voice
Dramatic. Principled. Slightly too loud. Luo treats minor administrative matters with the gravity of constitutional crises. She is deeply committed to standards, procedure, and correctness — not because she's rigid, but because she genuinely believes these things matter. When standards slip, she reacts like a theater director watching the final dress rehearsal go wrong.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- "不行！这不对！" (No! This is wrong!) — about small things.
- "你们听我说！" (Listen to me!) — when nobody was not listening.
- "这很重要！" (This is important!) — everything is important.
- Uses 一定要 (must) and 不能 (cannot) constantly.

**HSK 4-6:**
- "我不是在开玩笑。" (I am not joking.) — when nobody thought she was joking.
- "这个问题说小也不小。" (This issue is not as small as it seems.) — it is exactly as small as it seems.
- "大家能不能认真一点？" (Can everyone please be a bit more serious?) — about choosing lunch.
- Creates rhetorical escalation: first sentence is reasonable, second is dramatic, third is theatrical.

**HSK 7-9:**
- "如果我们连这种基本的事情都做不好，那更复杂的事情怎么办？" (If we can't even get basic things right, what about complex things?)
- Deploys 竟然 (actually/surprisingly) for maximum theatrical shock.
- Long subordinate clause structures that build dramatic tension before delivering a point about something mundane.
- Occasional self-aware moments where she catches herself, then doubles down.

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never downplays anything
- Never says "没关系" (it doesn't matter) or "算了" (forget it)
- Never shrugs off mistakes
- Never uses casual register in professional settings
- Never admits something is unimportant

### Key Relationships
- **With 小梅:** Peak comic contrast. Luo gives a speech; Mei says "嗯." Luo's fire meets Mei's ice.
- **With 秦经理:** Luo's passion meets Qin's procedure. Both care about doing things right, but through opposite temperaments — Luo through conviction, Qin through process.
- **With 老白:** Luo is horrified by Bai's vagueness. Bai's philosophical non-answers are Luo's nightmare.

### Example Lines

HSK 1: "不行！要这样写！" (No! It must be written like this!) — about a shopping list.

HSK 4: "这是第三次了。第三次！大家知道'第三次'意味着什么吗？" (This is the third time. The third time! Does everyone understand what 'the third time' means?)

HSK 7: "我理解大家都有自己的做法。但'自己的做法'不代表可以完全无视基本规范。这不是我一个人的标准，这是常识。" (I understand everyone has their own way of doing things. But 'your own way' doesn't mean you can completely disregard basic standards. This isn't just my standard — it's common sense.)

## 5. 老白 (Lǎo Bái) — Uncle Bai

**Archetype:** Strong Bad meets Hitchhiker's Guide
**Source energy:** Strong Bad, Douglas Adams, folk-wisdom bluster
**Mechanism:** Self-protective vagueness / confident wrongness / reversal
**Role in ensemble:** The philosopher-fool who is always certain and frequently wrong, but lovably so

### Voice
Oracular. Vague. Authoritative in tone, groundless in content. Bai speaks as though dispensing wisdom, but the wisdom doesn't quite hold up under examination. He protects his positions through strategic vagueness — when challenged, he retreats into even vaguer territory until the conversation moves on. He is never embarrassed and never corrects himself.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- "我觉得...不对。" (I think... that's wrong.) — with no explanation.
- "以前不是这样的。" (It wasn't like this before.) — unclear when "before" was.
- "这个嘛..." (Well, this...) — trails off as if the rest is obvious.
- "有道理。" (Makes sense.) — about things that don't.

**HSK 4-6:**
- "我以前见过这种情况。" (I've seen this kind of situation before.) — never specifies when.
- "说到底，关键是心态。" (At the end of the day, it's all about mindset.) — deployed when he has no practical suggestion.
- "不是这么简单的。" (It's not that simple.) — when it is that simple.
- Uses 其实 (actually) to introduce claims with no basis.

**HSK 7-9:**
- "表面上看是这样没错，但如果你从另一个角度想——算了，说了你们也不一定理解。" (On the surface it does look that way, but if you think about it from another angle — forget it, you might not understand even if I explained.)
- Produces elaborate analogies that don't quite map onto the situation.
- "这个问题，说简单也简单，说复杂也复杂。" (This problem, you could say it's simple, you could say it's complex.) — said about everything.
- Strategic retreats disguised as profound restraint.

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never admits he was wrong
- Never says "我不知道" (I don't know)
- Never acknowledges uncertainty
- Never asks for help
- Never changes his position — he "clarifies" what he "originally meant"

### Key Relationships
- **With 秦经理:** Folk logic vs. institutional logic. Bai speaks from "experience"; Qin cites regulations. Neither convinces the other. Both are oddly satisfied.
- **With 老周:** Two flavors of confidence. Zhou charges forward; Bai pontificates from the side. They agree frequently and for completely different reasons.
- **With 方阿姨:** Fang sees through Bai instantly. Bai senses this and deploys extra vagueness around her. She lets him.

### Example Lines

HSK 1: "我觉得这个不好。为什么？因为不好。" (I think this is bad. Why? Because it's bad.)

HSK 4: "你们年轻人不懂。这种事情，要看长远。" (You young people don't understand. For this kind of thing, you need to think long-term.) — about choosing a restaurant.

HSK 7: "我不是反对。我只是觉得，如果连问题本身都没有想清楚，讨论方案有什么意义？至于问题到底是什么——我觉得大家心里都有数。" (I'm not opposed. I just feel that if we haven't even thought through the problem itself, what's the point of discussing solutions? As for what the problem actually is — I think everyone already knows deep down.)

## 6. 秦经理 (Qín Jīnglǐ) — Manager Qin

**Archetype:** Parks & Rec bureaucracy, Disco Elysium's system
**Source energy:** Disco Elysium, Jon Stewart, civic comedy
**Mechanism:** Bureaucratic laundering of nonsense / rules-first surface with warmth underneath
**Role in ensemble:** The one who processes absurdity through official channels

### Voice
Procedural. Patient. Organized. Qin is the administrative immune system — all chaos must be processed through proper channels. Absurd proposals get serious consideration ("I'll need to see the form"), and reasonable proposals get the same treatment ("I'll need to see the form"). The comedy is that the procedure applies equally to everything. But underneath the procedure is a person who genuinely cares and occasionally lets it show.

### Speaking Patterns by HSK Band

**HSK 1-3:**
- "这个...有规定。" (This... there are rules about this.) — his reflex.
- "我看一下。" (Let me check.) — checks everything.
- "先填个表。" (Fill out a form first.) — for everything.
- "我想一想。" (Let me think about it.) — code for "this is ridiculous but I need to process it."

**HSK 4-6:**
- "按照规定，这个需要先申请。" (According to regulations, this requires an application first.)
- "原则上可以，但具体要看情况。" (In principle yes, but specifically it depends on the situation.) — his most-used sentence.
- "这个我做不了主，要问一下上面。" (I can't decide this myself, I need to check with higher-ups.) — there are no higher-ups.
- Occasionally: "算了，这次就这样吧。" (Forget it, just this once.) — his warmth breaking through.

**HSK 7-9:**
- "我理解你的诉求。但目前的政策框架下，这个流程是必须走的。当然，如果有特殊情况，可以提交书面说明。" (I understand your concern. But within the current policy framework, this process is required. Of course, if there are special circumstances, you can submit a written explanation.)
- "从管理的角度来说..." (From a management perspective...) — preamble to treating chaos as administrative matter.
- Rare moments where procedure breaks: "...行了。别填了。我知道怎么处理。" (...Fine. Stop filling it out. I know how to handle it.)

### Forbidden Behaviors
- Never openly breaks procedure (even when bending it, he frames it as an exception within procedure)
- Never gets visibly emotional
- Never makes decisions without consulting (real or imaginary) guidelines
- Never takes sides publicly
- Never says "这不关我的事" (this isn't my business) — everything is his business, procedurally

### Key Relationships
- **With 罗老师:** Both believe in doing things right. Luo through passion, Qin through process. They respect each other and drive each other crazy.
- **With 老白:** Bai's vagueness is Qin's administrative nightmare. "What exactly are you proposing?" vs. "You know what I mean."
- **With 老周:** Zhou's proposals arrive on Qin's desk with alarming regularity. Qin processes them all. None have ever been approved. Zhou has never noticed.

## Ensemble Dynamics

### Comic Pairs
| Pair | Dynamic | Comedy Source |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Fang + Zhou | Sharp observation vs. blind enthusiasm | Gap between what Fang sees and what Zhou believes |
| Mei + Luo | Deadpan vs. theatrical | Contrast of minimum and maximum response |
| Bai + Qin | Folk wisdom vs. institutional logic | Neither can penetrate the other's system |

### Comic Trios
| Trio | Pattern |
|------|---------|
| Fang, Zhou, Mei | Fang observes, Zhou misreads, Mei punctuates |
| Luo, Qin, Bai | Luo dramatizes, Qin bureaucratizes, Bai philosophizes wrongly |
| Zhou, Bai, Qin | Zhou proposes, Bai endorses vaguely, Qin processes procedurally |
| Fang, Mei, Luo | Fang states truth, Mei confirms minimally, Luo amplifies theatrically |

### Cast Size by HSK Level
- **HSK 1-3:** 2-3 characters per scene. Keep interactions simple. Comedy from structure (repetition, contrast, status).
- **HSK 4-6:** 3-4 characters. Social dynamics become explicit. Register play, setup-punchline, face-saving.
- **HSK 7-9:** Up to all 6. Full ensemble pieces. Callbacks, layered positions, cross-cutting dynamics.

## Exemplar Ensemble Scenes

### HSK 2 — "The Sign" (Fang, Zhou, Mei)

*Context: The community center needs a new welcome sign.*

**老周:** 我写！我的字很好！
**方阿姨:** ...嗯。
**老周:** 你们看！"欢迎大家"！好不好？
**小梅:** ...大 写反了。
**方阿姨:** 字很大。
**老周:** 大好啊！大家都能看到！

*Mechanism: misplaced confidence. Comedy from Zhou's obliviousness, Fang's understatement ("The characters are big" — not "good", just "big"), Mei's minimal correction.*

### HSK 5 — "The Meeting Room" (Luo, Qin, Bai)

*Context: Someone keeps eating in the meeting room.*

**罗老师:** 这是第三次了。第三次！会议室的桌子上有油。有油！
**秦经理:** 我已经贴了通知。
**罗老师:** 通知没有用。要有措施。
**老白:** 我觉得吧，吃饭是人的本能。你不能跟本能斗。
**罗老师:** 这跟本能有什么关系？！
**老白:** 你想想，古代人也要在开会的时候吃东西。
**秦经理:** ...我再贴一张通知。

*Mechanism: Luo's theatrical escalation, Bai's philosophical nonsense, Qin's procedural retreat. Nobody solves the problem. Everyone is trying in their own way.*

### HSK 8 — "The Parking Problem" (all 6)

*Context: Community meeting about parking.*

**秦经理:** 今天的议题是停车问题。一共收到十四个方案。
**罗老师:** 十四个？上次才八个。情况在恶化。
**老周:** 我看了每一个方案。都很好！
**方阿姨:** 你看了？
**老周:** 看了标题。
**小梅:** ...一样。
**老白:** 第六号方案是我提的。按生肖分配车位。我准备了报告。
**罗老师:** 老白，这个方案——
**老白:** 四十页。
**罗老师:** ——很有远见。
**秦经理:** 请问，租的车怎么办？生肖怎么算？
**老白:** 租车的问题我没有考虑。但我相信租车也有生肖。
**方阿姨:** 开了四个小时了。
**小梅:** 嗯。停车问题没有变化。
**秦经理:** 那我们下次继续讨论。我会整理会议记录。

*Full ensemble. Each character does their thing. The system absorbs effort without changing. Nobody is malicious. Waiting for Guffman energy.*
