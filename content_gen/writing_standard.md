# Aelu Gold-Star Writing Standard

**House rule:** Aelu writing must be easy for a tired learner to process, precise enough to teach, natural enough to trust, and alive enough to remember.

## Seven Dimensions

### 1. Economy (Strunk & White + Zinsser)
Nothing slack. No filler, no throat-clearing, no educational cotton batting. Every part pulls its weight.

### 2. Concreteness (Orwell + classic style)
The reader should picture what is being said. Scenes, actions, objects, consequences — not foggy abstractions.

### 3. Truthfulness (Orwell)
No jargon to blur thought. No inflated formality to fake authority. No synthetic warmth to cover weak reasoning. Say what is happening in words that do not hide it.

### 4. Reader-flow (Williams + Lanham)
Material arrives in the order the reader needs, not the order the writer thought it. The sentence structure helps the reader see who is doing what.

### 5. Naturalness (Zinsser + Pinker)
The sentence sounds like language used by a real mind, not by a committee or content engine.

### 6. Grace (Williams + Pinker)
Rhythm, proportion, emphasis. The prose has shape and ease — not ornament for ornament's sake.

### 7. Pedagogical Yield (Aelu-specific)
After reading this, does the learner know what to do next time? Does it teach?

## Scoring (0-3 each, 21 max)

- 0: fails the dimension
- 1: partly meets it
- 2: mostly meets it
- 3: fully meets it

**Thresholds:** 19-21 excellent. 15-18 good. 11-14 revise. 0-10 rebuild.

**Hard rule:** Pedagogical Yield below 2 cannot ship.

## Anti-patterns (automated scan targets)

### Economy
- filler: "actually", "basically", "really", "just", "very", "quite", "rather", "somewhat"
- throat-clearing: "It is worth noting", "It should be noted", "It goes without saying"
- redundant pairs: "each and every", "first and foremost", "actually and truly"
- over-explained transitions: "What I mean to say is", "In other words"

### Concreteness
- abstract fog: "situation", "aspect", "element", "concept", "experience" (when vague)
- telling not showing: "This reveals...", "This shows...", "This demonstrates..."

### Truthfulness
- hedge clusters: "perhaps", "maybe", "sort of", "in a way"
- fake precision: "approximately", "roughly speaking"
- euphemism: softening real distinctions

### Reader-flow
- buried topic sentences
- information arriving in writer's order, not reader's

### Naturalness
- translationese: "In that moment", "At that moment", "with regard to", "in terms of"
- over-formal: "one cannot help but", "irrespective of", "for the purpose of"
- essay voice: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion", "It is important to note"
- passive clutter: "it was decided that", "it can be seen that"

### Grace
- monotonous sentence length
- excessive em-dashes (>3 per passage)
- repetitive openers ("I", "I", "I" or "He", "He", "He")

### Pedagogical Yield
- feedback that doesn't explain WHY
- questions that test recall instead of comprehension
- explanations that describe without clarifying the distinction
