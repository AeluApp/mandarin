# Aesthetic Dimensions Reference — The Missing Eight

The system's narrative content honors warmth, wistfulness, and civic grounding (Ghibli, Your Name, Zeniba, Uematsu, Moombas). This document defines the 8 missing dimensions from the other half of the taste profile and maps each to comic mechanisms, source influences, and HSK-band guidance.

## The Eight Dimensions

### 1. Wit

**Definition:** Intelligence made compact. Observation compressed into a line that earns a smile through precision, not performance. The gap between what is said and what is meant, closed by the audience.

**Before:** "他不想去，但是他不知道怎么说不。" (He didn't want to go, but he didn't know how to say no.)
**After:** "他说'再看看吧'。大家都知道了。" (He said 'let me think about it.' Everyone understood.)

**Comic mechanisms:** Understatement, polite aggression, reversal
**Source influences:** Seinfeld (observation as comedy), Tina Fey (compression), Bourdain (edge in noticing)
**Characters:** Fang (polite aggression), Mei (understatement)

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Wit through structure (repetition, contrast). Simple words, surprising juxtaposition.
- HSK 4-6: Wit through implication. 了 and 但是 unlock setup-punchline. Social inference.
- HSK 7-9: Full wit. Euphemism, register play, multi-layered implications.

### 2. Character Personality

**Definition:** Distinct, consistent, recognizable voice. A character's lines could not be spoken by any other character. Personality expressed through word choice, rhythm, and what they notice — not through self-description.

**Before:** NPC: "你好！今天天气很好。" (Hello! The weather is nice today.)
**After:** 方阿姨: "你来了。" Pause. Nothing else. (You're here.) — Fang's greeting is the absence of greeting. That IS her personality.

**Comic mechanisms:** All — each character has one mechanism that IS their personality
**Source influences:** Golden Girls (4 distinct voices), Arrested Development (character consistency), Christopher Guest (character revealed through behavior)
**Characters:** All 6 cast members

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Personality through patterns. Each character has signature phrases.
- HSK 4-6: Personality through what they notice, what they ignore, how they frame problems.
- HSK 7-9: Personality through register, argument structure, and relationship to evidence.

### 3. Ensemble Dynamics

**Definition:** Comedy that arises from the interaction between distinct personalities, not from any single character. The friction, harmony, and misalignment between people who see the world differently but must share a space.

**Before:** Two people have a conversation. One asks, one answers. Information exchanged.
**After:** Zhou proposes something impossible. Fang says "嗯" in a way that means "no." Mei states the actual problem in four words. Nobody's plan changes. The scene is funnier than any individual line.

**Comic mechanisms:** Status drop, misplaced confidence + understatement (in contrast), polite aggression
**Source influences:** Christopher Guest (ensemble as comedy), Arrested Development (family dynamics), Fire Emblem Three Houses (positions in tension)
**Characters:** All pairings and trios (see character_bible.md)

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: 2 characters, one clear contrast (e.g., enthusiast + deadpan).
- HSK 4-6: 3 characters, dynamic triangle. Each brings something different.
- HSK 7-9: Up to 6. Cross-cutting dynamics, callbacks, layered disagreements.

### 4. Smart Comedy

**Definition:** Comedy that respects the audience's intelligence. The joke requires one step of inference from the audience. Nothing is explained. Nothing is underlined. The humor lives in the gap the audience fills.

**Before:** "他做的菜很难吃，但是他觉得自己做得很好。这很好笑。" (His cooking was terrible, but he thought it was great. This is funny.) — telling us it's funny.
**After:** "老周做了菜。大家说'很特别'。老周又做了一次。" (Zhou cooked. Everyone said 'very unique.' Zhou cooked again.) — the audience fills in: "unique" was diplomacy, and Zhou took it as praise.

**Comic mechanisms:** Misplaced confidence, self-protective vagueness, euphemism
**Source influences:** Arrested Development (the audience knows more), Seinfeld (observation), Mel Brooks (precision in absurdity)
**Characters:** Zhou (confidence without basis), Bai (vagueness as strategy)

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: One-step inference. What is left unsaid is obvious from context.
- HSK 4-6: Social inference. Reading between lines. Understanding what "很特别" really means.
- HSK 7-9: Multi-layered inference. Unreliable narration, competing frames, euphemism chains.

### 5. Political/Moral Texture

**Definition:** Reasonable people disagreeing about real things. Both positions defensible. No villain, no moral lesson, no resolution forced. The texture of genuine complexity presented without taking sides.

**Before:** "保护环境很重要。" (Protecting the environment is important.) — thesis statement, no friction.
**After:** "老陈的茶馆要拆了。地铁出口要建在那里。老陈说这条巷子两百年了。秦经理说每天四千人从那个出口走。两个数字都是真的。" — two truths in tension, no resolution.

**Comic mechanisms:** Bureaucratic laundering of nonsense (when institutions process human problems), polite aggression (when disagreement is polite but real)
**Source influences:** Fire Emblem Three Houses (positions in genuine conflict), Disco Elysium (systems vs. humans), Bourdain (seeing all sides of a food culture), Jon Stewart (moral clarity without simplification)
**Characters:** Qin (institutional logic), Fang (uncomfortable truths), all characters in "moral/political texture" dialogues

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Simple two-position conflicts. Both sides stated plainly.
- HSK 4-6: Reasons behind positions become explicit. Register and face-saving add complexity.
- HSK 7-9: Full complexity. Historical context, institutional dynamics, competing goods.

### 6. Theatrical Energy

**Definition:** Disproportionate emotional investment in small things. Not mockery — genuine commitment that happens to be aimed at something minor. The comedy is in the sincerity, not in stupidity.

**Before:** "他很生气。" (He was angry.)
**After:** "罗老师站起来了。'这是第三次。第三次！'" (Teacher Luo stood up. 'This is the third time. The third time!') — about someone eating in the meeting room.

**Comic mechanisms:** Over-formality, status drop (when theatrical stakes meet trivial subject)
**Source influences:** Sondheim (commitment to the bit), Joan Cusack (theatrical sincerity), Avenue Q (disproportionate energy)
**Characters:** Luo (theatrical stakes for everything)

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Theatrical energy through repetition and exclamation. Simple vocabulary, big feeling.
- HSK 4-6: Rhetorical escalation. Subordinate clauses building dramatic tension about small things.
- HSK 7-9: Full theatrical argumentation applied to trivial matters. Self-aware moments.

### 7. Sharp Observation

**Definition:** Noticing with edge. Not just seeing — seeing AND having an opinion about what you see. The observer has a point of view. The observation reveals something about the world that is true and slightly uncomfortable.

**Before:** "面店的老板不太说话。" (The noodle shop owner doesn't talk much.)
**After:** "他从来不说'你好'。他只看我一眼。我坐下来。他给我端面。有一次我说'谢谢'，他看起来很奇怪，好像我说了一句不应该说的话。我觉得不说话是他的'你好'。" — the observation has a conclusion: silence IS the greeting. The observer noticed something most people wouldn't.

**Comic mechanisms:** Understatement, literal-mindedness (stating exactly what is observed)
**Source influences:** Bourdain (noticing with opinion), Seinfeld (observation as content), Strong Bad (noticing the absurd in the normal)
**Characters:** Fang (observes precisely), Mei (states what she sees without padding)

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Observations through concrete detail. What is seen, heard, counted.
- HSK 4-6: Observations that draw conclusions. "I think..." applied to something specific.
- HSK 7-9: Observations that reframe. The reader's understanding of the situation changes.

### 8. Affectionate Satire

**Definition:** Ridiculous but lovable. The system absorbs human effort without changing. Nobody is malicious — everyone is trying. The comedy is structural, not personal. The audience laughs WITH, not AT.

**Before:** "会议开了很久。" (The meeting went on a long time.)
**After:** "小区委员会开了紧急会议。一共十四个方案。第六号方案建议按生肖分配车位。会议开了四个小时。停车问题没有变化。" — everyone tried. Nothing changed. Affection is in the detail (fourteen plans, zodiac parking, four hours).

**Comic mechanisms:** Bureaucratic laundering of nonsense, excessive earnestness, misplaced confidence
**Source influences:** Christopher Guest (Waiting for Guffman, Best in Show — lovable incompetence), Mel Brooks (affection for the ridiculous), Parks & Rec (government that tries), Strong Bad (systems that fail lovably)
**Characters:** Zhou (earnest failure), Qin (procedural absorption), Bai (confident wrongness), all 6 in full ensemble

**HSK guidance:**
- HSK 1-3: Simple satire. Something tried, something failed, affection preserved.
- HSK 4-6: Institutional satire. Meetings, plans, reports, procedures that absorb effort.
- HSK 7-9: Full Waiting for Guffman. Complex systems, detailed plans, documented failures, nobody gives up.

## Integration Notes

- These 8 dimensions supplement, not replace, the existing warm/wistful/civic aesthetic.
- Not every piece of content needs all 8. Most need 1-2.
- The character cast is the primary vehicle for injecting these dimensions.
- All content must still pass all 6 gold standards simultaneously.
- The goal is range, not replacement. The system should feel like it has a full emotional palette, not just the warm half.
