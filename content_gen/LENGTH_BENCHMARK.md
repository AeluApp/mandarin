Research date: 2026-03-07

## 1. Passage Length: Competitor & Standard Benchmarks

### Du Chinese (graded reader app, 3,000+ lessons)

Du Chinese organizes by vocabulary level (unique characters the story draws from), not by passage character count directly. Individual lessons range from "a couple hundred to a thousand characters" on screen.

| Level | Vocab Pool | Estimated Passage Length |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| Newbie (HSK 1) | ~150 unique chars | 100-250 chars per lesson |
| Elementary (HSK 2) | ~300 unique chars | 200-400 chars |
| Intermediate (HSK 3) | ~600 unique chars | 300-600 chars |
| Upper-Intermediate (HSK 4) | ~1,000 unique chars | 400-800 chars |
| Advanced (HSK 5) | ~2,500 unique chars | 600-1,000 chars |
| Master (HSK 6) | full range | 800-1,200+ chars |

Audio runs 1-2 minutes at intermediate; stories span 1-5 iPhone screens.

Sources: [LTL School review](https://ltl-school.com/du-chinese/), [DigMandarin review](https://www.digmandarin.com/duchinese-review.html), [Du Chinese blog](https://duchinese.net/blog/2019/12/16/80-an-introduction-to-chinese-graded-readers/)

### The Chairman's Bao (news-based graded reading)

TCB rewrites real news articles at each HSK level. Articles get progressively longer. Specific character counts are not publicly documented, but reviews describe:

- HSK 1-2: Short paragraphs, heavily simplified
- HSK 3-4: Medium articles, several paragraphs
- HSK 5-6+: Full news articles approaching native length

Each article includes 4-6 comprehension questions.

Sources: [AllLanguageResources review](https://www.alllanguageresources.com/chairmans-bao-review/), [TCB blog](https://www.thechairmansbao.com/blog/brand-new-feature-comprehension-questions-learn-chinese/)

### Mandarin Companion (graded reader books)

These are full novellas, not short passages, but useful for understanding total reading volume expectations:

| Level | Unique Chars | Total Book Length |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Breakthrough | 150 | ~5,000 chars (~60 pages) |
| Level 1 | 300 | ~10,000 chars (~90 pages) |
| Level 2 | 450 | ~10,000 chars (~90 pages) |

Individual chapters run roughly 500-1,000 chars at Breakthrough, 800-1,500 chars at Level 1.

Sources: [Mandarin Companion](https://mandarincompanion.com/), [Hacking Chinese review](https://www.hackingchinese.com/review-mandarin-companion-graded-readers-level-1/)

Sources: [GlobalExam HSK 3](https://global-exam.com/blog/en/hsk-3-reading-comprehension/), [PrePEdu HSK 4](https://prepedu.com/en/blog/hsk-4-reading-practice)

### CEFR-Aligned Recommendations

CEFR does not prescribe specific passage lengths. Difficulty is determined by linguistic complexity (lexical diversity, syntactic depth, word familiarity) rather than character count. However, in practice:

- A1-A2: Short texts, 50-200 words (signs, menus, simple messages)
- B1: 200-500 words (emails, short articles)
- B2: 500-800 words (articles, reports)
- C1-C2: 800-1,500+ words (academic texts, literary passages)

For reference, TOEFL iBT uses ~700-word passages with 10 questions each.

Source: [Cambridge CEFR](https://www.cambridgeenglish.org/exams-and-tests/cefr/)

### Synthesis: Industry-Standard Passage Lengths for App-Based Graded Readers

Combining all sources, the consensus range for **individual reading passages in apps** (not full books):

| Level | Recommended Chars | Rationale |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| HSK 1 | 50-120 | Du Chinese Newbie range; HSK exam uses 30-80 |
| HSK 2 | 100-200 | Elementary graded readers; HSK exam short paragraphs |
| HSK 3 | 150-350 | HSK exam Part III 200-300; Du Chinese ~300-600 but those are full stories |
| HSK 4 | 250-450 | HSK exam 200-300; textbook passages longer |
| HSK 5 | 400-700 | HSK exam 600-1,000; textbook 600-700 per lesson |
| HSK 6 | 500-900 | Approaching native article length |
| HSK 7-9 | 600-1,200+ | Native-level long-form reading |

## 2. Dialogue Length: Turns & Player Choice Points

### Competitor Benchmarks

**Pimsleur Mandarin:**
- 30-minute audio lessons built around a short dialogue (4-8 turns)
- Dialogue is broken down and practiced; learner repeats/recalls
- No branching or choice points

**ChinesePod:**
- Elementary: "very short dialogue" (~4-6 turns), 15-minute lesson
- Intermediate: longer dialogues (~8-12 turns), more Chinese used
- Advanced: "crazy long" dialogues (~15-20+ turns)

**HelloChinese:**
- Gamified format, short dialogues (~3-6 turns at beginner)
- Interactive fill-in-the-blank within dialogue frames
- Lessons 10-15 minutes

**Duolingo (general reference):**
- Dialogue exercises use 2-4 choice points per conversation
- 3 options per choice (1 correct, 2 distractors)

### Language Pedagogy Research

Research on dialogue-based CALL (Computer-Assisted Language Learning) shows:
- Structured dialogue practice has "significant medium effect size" on L2 proficiency
- Packed practice (concentrated sessions) shows higher outcomes than spread-out practice
- Meaningful conversational context matters more than dialogue length
- Branching scenarios with 3 options per choice point (1 correct, 2 distractors) is the established standard for dialogue simulations

Source: [ResearchGate meta-analysis](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370635300_Dialogue_systems_for_language_learning_A_meta-analysis)

### Synthesis: Recommended Dialogue Parameters

| Level | Total Turns | Player Choice Points | Notes |
|-------|------------|---------------------|-------|
| HSK 1 | 4-6 | 2-3 | Simple transactional exchanges |
| HSK 2 | 5-8 | 2-3 | Slightly longer, still formulaic |
| HSK 3 | 6-10 | 3-4 | Introducing topic shifts, opinions |
| HSK 4 | 8-12 | 3-5 | Extended conversation, negotiation |
| HSK 5 | 10-14 | 4-6 | Complex topics, register shifts |
| HSK 6 | 12-16 | 5-7 | Debate, persuasion, nuance |
| HSK 7-9 | 12-20 | 5-8 | Native-level extended discourse |

## 3. Comprehension Questions Per Passage

### Competitor Benchmarks

| Source | Questions Per Passage | Format |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| The Chairman's Bao | 4-6 per article | True/false, sentence ordering, vocab matching, grammar, Q&A |
| Du Chinese | 0-3 per lesson | Tap-word definitions; some lessons have quiz questions |
| HSK 3 Exam | 1 per short passage | MC (3 options) |
| HSK 4 Exam | ~1 per passage | MC (4 options) |
| HSK 5 Exam | 4-5 per long passage | MC (4 options) |
| TOEFL iBT | 10 per passage (~700 words) | MC, insert text, summary |
| IELTS Reading | ~13 per passage (~900 words) | Various (matching, T/F/NG, MC, completion) |

### Synthesis: Recommended Questions Per Passage

| Passage Length | Recommended Questions | Rationale |
|---------------|----------------------|-----------|
| < 100 chars | 1-2 | Short passage, 1 factual + 1 inference max |
| 100-300 chars | 2-3 | Room for detail retrieval + inference |
| 300-500 chars | 3-4 | TCB standard; enough material for varied question types |
| 500-800 chars | 4-5 | HSK 5 exam standard |
| 800+ chars | 5-6 | Approaching TCB/test standards for long-form |

## 5. Gap Analysis & Recommendations

### Passage Length Verdict

| Level | Current Avg | Industry Target | Status | Action |
|-------|-------------|----------------|--------|--------|
| HSK 1 | 66 chars | 50-120 | GOOD | Within range |
| HSK 2 | 98 chars | 100-200 | GOOD | At low end; could push some passages to 150-200 |
| HSK 3 | 194 chars | 150-350 | GOOD | Solid middle of range |
| HSK 4 | 250 chars | 250-450 | LOW-END | Could push avg to 300-350; max only 288 is tight |
| HSK 5 | 286 chars | 400-700 | SHORT | Significantly below target. HSK 5 exam uses 600-1,000. Need 400-600 avg. |
| HSK 6 | 378 chars | 500-900 | SHORT | Below target. Need 500-700 avg. |
| HSK 7 | 409 chars | 600-1,200 | SHORT | Should be 600-800 avg minimum |
| HSK 8 | 462 chars | 600-1,200 | SHORT | Should be 700-900 avg |
| HSK 9 | 514 chars | 600-1,200 | SHORT | Should be 800-1,100 avg |

**Key finding:** HSK 1-3 passages are well-calibrated. HSK 4 is borderline. HSK 5-9 passages are consistently 30-50% shorter than industry norms. The gap widens at higher levels.

**Recommendation:** For HSK 5+, passages should roughly double in average length. This is the single biggest content gap. At HSK 5, the HSK Standard Course textbook uses 600-700 character articles per lesson. Our average of 286 is less than half that.

### Comprehension Questions Verdict

| Level | Current Avg | Recommended | Status | Action |
|-------|-------------|-------------|--------|--------|
| HSK 1 | 2.8 | 1-2 | OVER | Could trim to 2; short passages don't support 3 meaningful questions |
| HSK 2 | 3.0 | 2-3 | GOOD | Top of range; fine |
| HSK 3 | 2.8 | 2-3 | GOOD | Appropriate |
| HSK 4 | 2.8 | 3-4 | LOW | Should add 1-2 more questions as passages get longer |
| HSK 5 | 2.4 | 3-4 | LOW | Should be 3-4 (especially once passages are lengthened) |
| HSK 6 | 2.1 | 4-5 | LOW | Significantly under; TCB uses 4-6 |
| HSK 7 | 2.6 | 4-5 | LOW | Same issue |
| HSK 8 | 2.5 | 4-5 | LOW | Same issue |
| HSK 9 | 2.1 | 5-6 | LOW | Long passages deserve more questions |

**Key finding:** Question counts are inversely correlated with level -- the opposite of what they should be. HSK 1 has the most questions (2.8) while HSK 9 has the fewest (2.1). Industry practice is the reverse: longer passages get more questions.

**Recommendation:** Increase question counts at HSK 5+ to 3-5 per passage. Vary question types (add inference, vocabulary-in-context, sentence ordering, true/false alongside MC). Consider reducing HSK 1 to 2 questions per passage.

### Dialogue Length Verdict

| Level | Current Turns | Target | Status | Action |
|-------|--------------|--------|--------|--------|
| HSK 1 | 4.4 | 4-6 | GOOD | Right in range |
| HSK 2 | 4.4 | 5-8 | LOW-END | Could push to 5-6 avg |
| HSK 3 | 6.0 | 6-10 | GOOD | At low end but acceptable |
| HSK 4 | 6.2 | 8-12 | SHORT | Should be 8-10 avg |
| HSK 5 | 7.1 | 10-14 | SHORT | Should be 10-12 avg |
| HSK 6 | 7.2 | 12-16 | SHORT | Should be 10-14 avg |
| HSK 7 | 6.4 | 12-20 | SHORT | Major gap; fewer turns than HSK 3 |
| HSK 8 | 5.4 | 12-20 | SHORT | Regression; shorter than HSK 2 |
| HSK 9 | 5.7 | 12-20 | SHORT | Same regression problem |

**Key finding:** Dialogue turns peak at HSK 5-6 (7.1-7.2) then regress at HSK 7-9 (5.4-6.4). This is backwards. Higher-level dialogues should be longer and more complex, not shorter.

**Recommendation:**
- HSK 4-6: Extend dialogues to 8-14 turns average
- HSK 7-9: These need the most work. Extend to 12-18 turns with 5-8 player choice points. Advanced dialogues should involve extended negotiation, debate, storytelling, or multi-topic conversations.
- Player choice points scale appropriately at HSK 1-6 but the HSK 7-9 regression (2.7-3.2 choices vs. HSK 6's 3.5) needs correction.

## 6. Priority Actions (ranked by impact)

1. **Lengthen HSK 5-9 passages** -- Current passages are 30-50% shorter than industry norms. This is the biggest gap. Target: HSK 5 avg 500 chars, HSK 6 avg 650, HSK 7 avg 750, HSK 8 avg 850, HSK 9 avg 1,000.

2. **Fix HSK 7-9 dialogue regression** -- Turns and player choices decrease at advanced levels instead of increasing. Target: 12-18 turns, 5-8 choices.

3. **Add more comprehension questions to HSK 5-9 passages** -- Currently 2.1-2.5; should be 3-5. Add varied question types (inference, vocab-in-context, true/false).

4. **Extend HSK 4-6 dialogues** -- Push from 6-7 turns to 8-14 turns.

5. **Increase HSK 7-9 dialogue volume** -- 20 dialogues per level is thin compared to 68-110 at lower levels.

6. **Minor: Trim HSK 1 questions to 2** -- Short passages at this level don't meaningfully support 3 questions.
