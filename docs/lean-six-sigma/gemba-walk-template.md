# Gemba Walk Template — Aelu User Observation

**Owner:** Jason Gerson
**Created:** 2026-03-10
**Purpose:** Structured observation of real users interacting with Aelu. Go to where the work happens — watch the learner learn.

---

## 1. Pre-Observation Setup

### Logistics
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes (1 full session + debrief)
- **Setting:** User's natural study environment (home, office, cafe) — remote via screen share is acceptable
- **Recording:** Screen recording + audio (with consent)
- **Observer role:** Silent. Do not help, hint, explain, or react. Write notes. You are a fly on the wall.

### Equipment Checklist
- [ ] Screen recording software active (or Zoom/Meet screen share recording)
- [ ] Audio recording (for unprompted comments)
- [ ] Observation sheet (section 4 below) printed or open
- [ ] Timer (to measure durations at observation points)
- [ ] Consent form signed (section 3 below)

### Pre-Session Questions (ask before they start)
1. When did you last study Mandarin? (context: are they returning after a gap?)
2. How are you feeling about studying right now? (baseline emotional state)
3. How long do you plan to study today? (expected vs. actual comparison)

## 2. Ten Observation Points

### (a) Time to First Drill After Login

**What to observe:**
- How long between opening the app and answering the first drill question?
- Does the user pause on the dashboard? Read anything? Or go straight to "Start Session"?
- Any confusion about where to click?

**What to record:**
- Timestamp: app opens at _____, first drill answer at _____
- Elapsed time: _____ seconds
- Dashboard interactions (if any): _____
- Navigation confusion (Y/N): _____

**Why it matters:** Every second between login and first drill is non-learning time. If users consistently spend 30+ seconds on the dashboard, it may be over-designed.

---

### (b) Hesitation Before Answering

**What to observe:**
- How long does the user pause before typing/clicking an answer?
- Do they mouth the word silently? Look away? Count on fingers (for tone)?
- Is there a pattern: do they hesitate more on certain drill types?

**What to record:**
- Drill type when longest hesitation occurred: _____
- Estimated hesitation (seconds): _____
- Observable behavior during hesitation: _____
- Did they use the ? (half-credit) or N (don't know) shortcuts? _____

**Why it matters:** Long hesitation on MC drills may indicate distractors are too close (good) or the user is confused by the drill format (bad). On free-text drills, hesitation correlates with difficulty.

---

### (c) Use of Audio Playback

**What to observe:**
- Does the user click the audio play button?
- Do they listen more than once?
- Do they try to mouth along with the audio?
- Do they ever play audio and then change their answer?

**What to record:**
- Audio plays per session: _____
- Drills where audio was played: _____
- Re-listens (>1 play): _____
- Answer changed after audio (Y/N): _____

**Why it matters:** If users never play audio, either they don't need it (advanced) or the button is not discoverable. If they always re-listen, TTS quality or speed may be an issue.

---

### (d) Reading Context Notes

**What to observe:**
- When a context note is displayed, does the user read it or scroll past?
- How long do they spend on it?
- Do they nod, make a sound, or show any recognition?

**What to record:**
- Context notes shown: _____
- Context notes visibly read (user paused, eyes tracked): _____
- Context notes skipped: _____
- Average time on context note: _____ seconds
- Any verbal reaction: _____

**Why it matters:** Context notes are a significant content investment (299 notes). If users skip them, they're inventory waste.

---

### (e) Reaction to Incorrect Grade

**What to observe:**
- What does the user do immediately after seeing a wrong answer?
- Do they read the feedback? Study the correct answer?
- Emotional reaction: frustration, surprise, indifference, self-correction?
- Do they say anything out loud? ("Oh right," "That's not fair," "What?")

**What to record:**
- Total incorrect answers observed: _____
- Reactions categorized:
  - Read feedback carefully: _____
  - Glanced at feedback, moved on: _____
  - Showed frustration (sigh, headshake): _____
  - Showed surprise: _____
  - Verbalized disagreement with grading: _____
  - No visible reaction: _____

**Why it matters:** If users don't read feedback, the error classification and elaborated feedback are wasted effort. If they disagree with grading, the grading logic has a perceived fairness problem (even if technically correct).

---

### (f) Session Exit Point

**What to observe:**
- Does the user complete the full session or quit early?
- If early exit: at what item number did they quit?
- What happened right before the exit? (A streak of wrong answers? A distraction? A specific drill type?)
- Did they look at the clock or their phone before quitting?

**What to record:**
- Session completed (Y/N): _____
- Items completed / items planned: _____ / _____
- If early exit, last drill type before exit: _____
- Last 3 drill results before exit (C/W): _____
- Observable trigger for exit: _____

**Why it matters:** Directly validates the early session abandonment root cause analysis. Identifying the specific moment of quitting reveals the trigger.

---

### (g) Navigation Confusion

**What to observe:**
- Does the user ever click the wrong thing?
- Do they look lost — scanning the page for the next action?
- Do they use the browser back button (indicating they went somewhere unintended)?
- Do they ask "where do I...?" or "how do I...?" (even in self-talk)

**What to record:**
- Wrong clicks: _____
- Back button uses: _____
- Visible scanning/searching behavior: _____
- Verbal questions about navigation: _____
- Specific areas of confusion: _____

**Why it matters:** Navigation confusion is motion waste. Every wrong click is friction that accumulates toward abandonment.

---

### (h) Feature Discovery

**What to observe:**
- Does the user notice and use any features beyond basic drilling?
- Graded reader? Media shelf? Listening practice? Focus command? Settings?
- Do they hover over or click on anything out of curiosity?
- Do they discover the ? (half-credit) or N (I don't know) shortcuts?

**What to record:**
- Features used voluntarily: _____
- Features noticed but not used: _____
- Features apparently not discovered: _____
- ? or N shortcut discovery (Y/N): _____
- Approximate time to discover shortcuts: _____

**Why it matters:** Features that users never discover are invisible inventory. The ? and N shortcuts are important pedagogical tools — if users don't know about them, sessions are more frustrating than necessary.

---

### (i) Emotional State Changes

**What to observe:**
- Does the user's emotional state change during the session?
- Track visible emotions: focused, relaxed, frustrated, bored, engaged, amused, proud
- When do transitions happen? (After a correct streak? After a hard question? After audio?)
- Does the overall arc go up (starts anxious, ends satisfied) or down (starts eager, ends frustrated)?

**What to record:**
- Emotional state at start: _____
- State at mid-session (item 7-8): _____
- State at end: _____
- Key transition moments:
  - Drill #___: emotion shifted from _____ to _____ because _____
  - Drill #___: emotion shifted from _____ to _____ because _____

**Why it matters:** Emotional trajectory predicts return behavior. Sessions that end on a frustration note reduce next-session likelihood. The difficulty pacing countermeasure (from root cause analysis) aims to ensure sessions end on a positive note.

---

### (j) Unprompted Comments

**What to observe:**
- Anything the user says out loud during the session without being asked
- Self-talk about the content ("Oh, I know this one," "Wait, that's not right")
- Opinions about the system ("This is cool," "Why is it asking me this?")
- Complaints, suggestions, confusion expressed verbally

**What to record:**
- Verbatim quotes (capture exact words):
  1. "_____" (context: drill #___, type: _____)
  2. "_____" (context: _____)
  3. "_____" (context: _____)
- Categorize each:
  - Content reaction: _____
  - System reaction: _____
  - Self-assessment: _____
  - Feature request: _____
  - Complaint: _____

**Why it matters:** Unprompted comments are the most honest user feedback. They reveal what users actually think, not what they say when asked.

## 5. Debrief Questions (ask after the session)

Ask these within 5 minutes of session completion, while the experience is fresh:

1. **How was that?** (open-ended, let them talk first)
2. **Was there a moment where you felt stuck or confused?** What happened?
3. **Was there a moment where something clicked or felt satisfying?**
4. **Did any drill feel unfair — like you got it right but the system said wrong?**
5. **Was the session the right length?** Too long, too short, or about right?
6. **Is there anything you wished the app would do that it didn't?**
7. **On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to study again tomorrow?** Why that number?

## 7. Observation Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Full Gemba walk (30-45 min) | Monthly with VoC interviews | Combine with interview when possible |
| Self-observation (Jason as user) | Every session | Abbreviated — just note friction points |
| Remote screen share observation | As users become available | Lower barrier — user stays in their environment |
| In-person observation | Quarterly if possible | Richest data — see posture, environment, facial expressions |
