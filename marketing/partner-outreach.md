## Outreach Strategy

### Sequencing
1. **Cold outreach**: Send Template A (the first-touch email)
2. **Follow-up**: If no response after 7 days, send Template B (shorter, one-line ask)
3. **No second follow-up**: If they don't respond to Template B, move on. Never chase.

### Finding Email Addresses
- Check their "About" page, YouTube channel description, or blog contact page
- Many creators list a business email in their YouTube "About" tab
- If no email: DM on the platform where they're most active
- If no DM: leave a thoughtful comment on a recent video/post, mentioning you'd love to chat

### Volume Plan
- **Week 1**: Outreach to 10 prospects (mix of categories)
- **Week 2**: Follow up on Week 1 non-responders, outreach to 10 new
- **Week 3-4**: Continue at 5-10 per week
- **Target**: 40-50 outreach attempts in first month
- **Expected response rate**: 15-25% (6-12 responses)
- **Expected partnership rate**: 30-50% of responders (2-6 active partners)

## Rejection Handling

**If they say "not interested right now":**
> Totally understand. The free account is yours to keep if you ever want to try it. Thanks for considering it.

**If they say "I don't do affiliate promotions":**
> Completely respect that. The offer stands as just a free account with no promotion expected. If you ever find it useful and want to mention it organically, great — but zero obligation.

**If they say "I'd prefer a flat fee for a sponsored video":**
> I appreciate the transparency. We're an independent team and don't have a sponsorship budget right now, but we'd love to revisit this when we're further along. In the meantime, the free account and affiliate option are available if either appeals to you.

**If they say "send me the app and I'll review it honestly":**
> Perfect — that's exactly what I want. Here's your account: [credentials]. Take your time with it. Honest feedback (even critical) is more valuable to me than a promotional mention.

**If they ghost:**
> Send Template B after 7 days. If still no response, move on. Never send a third message.
