What Anki does well

Anki is a remarkable tool. If you are technically proficient and willing to invest setup time, it can serve you for years.

Free and open source $0 on desktop, web, and Android. $25 one-time on iOS. The economics are unbeatable. No subscription, no lock-in.
FSRS algorithm The canonical implementation of the state-of-the-art scheduling algorithm. Proven over millions of users and years of research.
Infinite customization Card templates, add-ons, plugins, media embedding. If you can imagine it, you can build it. No other SRS tool offers this flexibility.
Massive ecosystem Thousands of shared Chinese decks, community guides, add-on developers. A 15-year-old ecosystem that keeps growing.

Where Anki falls short for Chinese

Setup overhead Finding a good Chinese deck requires research. Many shared decks have errors, bad audio, or poor example sentences. Making good cards takes longer than studying them.
One drill type: flashcards Front/back cards test recognition. They do not test whether you can hear a word, produce it, use it in context, or distinguish its tone from a near-miss.
No reading integration Anki cannot connect your reading to your reviews. Every word you encounter while reading Chinese must be manually turned into a card.
No curriculum Anki does not tell you what to study next. You supply the content and the order. For Chinese, where HSK progression matters, this is a gap.

What $14.99/month buys you over Anki

No setup time Open the app, start a session. HSK-aligned content is curated, annotated, and ready. No decks to find, vet, or maintain.
27 drill types instead of 1 Cloze deletion, sentence construction, listening comprehension, tone pair discrimination, character recognition, speaking drills with tone grading, and more.
The cleanup loop Read graded passages, tap words you do not know, and those words automatically enter your SRS queue. Anki cannot do this.
Tone grading Record yourself and get feedback on tone accuracy. Anki has no concept of pronunciation assessment.
Multi-skill diagnostics The system tracks listening, vocabulary, reading, and tones separately. It knows your listening lags behind your vocabulary and adjusts accordingly.
A curriculum that decides for you Aelu sequences content by HSK level, adjusts based on your performance data, and handles interleaving. You study. The system plans.

Who should use which

Use Anki if you...

  • Enjoy building and customizing your own study system
  • Study multiple languages or subjects alongside Chinese
  • Have invested years in Chinese Anki decks you do not want to leave
  • Need specialized vocabulary we do not cover (medical, legal, dialect)
  • Cannot pay $14.99/month and have time to invest in setup
  • Want total control over every aspect of your SRS

Use Aelu if you...

  • Want to study Chinese, not maintain a flashcard system
  • Want reading and drilling connected in one workflow
  • Want tone grading and listening drills built in
  • Want to know which skills are lagging, not just which cards are due
  • Are preparing for HSK and want aligned content
  • Value your study time more than your setup time

Honest take

Anki is free. Anki has the canonical FSRS implementation. Anki's ecosystem is vastly larger. If you are technically proficient and willing to invest setup time, Anki plus a good deck plus supplementary reading tools can replicate much of what Aelu does, for free.

Our advantage is integration and ease-of-use, not raw capability. Aelu is for the learner who wants to sit down and practice Chinese without thinking about deck management, card templates, or plugin configuration.

If you enjoy the meta-game of optimizing Anki, you will probably stay with Anki. If you want to open an app and study, Aelu is worth $14.99/month.
