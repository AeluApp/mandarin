Getting Started

Mandarin is an adaptive Chinese learning app with 27 drill types, graded reading, listening practice, speaking drills with tone grading, and honest per-skill diagnostics. The core idea: when you read Chinese in the app and look up a word, that word automatically becomes multi-skill practice. It is built by learners, for learners.

The free tier includes all HSK 1-2 content, all 27 drill types, graded reading, and listening practice. It is not a trial and there is no time limit. If HSK 1-2 is where you are, use it for as long as you need. No credit card required to start.

Duolingo has roughly 3 exercise formats and optimizes for daily engagement. Anki is a powerful blank SRS engine where you build your own curriculum. HelloChinese is a solid guided app with about 6 drill types.

Mandarin has 27 drill types, a graded reader with the cleanup loop (every word you look up becomes practice), per-skill diagnostics tracking vocabulary, listening, reading, and tones independently, and FSRS-based scheduling with Bayesian confidence dampening. No gamification, no XP, no streaks. The system targets what you are weakest at, not what is easiest to test.

Yes. Mandarin is a web app that works in any modern browser on desktop, tablet, or phone. You can add it to your home screen and it behaves like a native app. No download, no app store, no storage space needed. It also works offline.

When you read a graded passage and tap a word you do not know, you get the definition inline. That word is automatically added to your drill queue. Your next study session focuses on those exact words -- not through one flashcard format, but across multiple drill types: tone discrimination, cloze deletion, audio matching, sentence construction, and more. The loop: encounter, look up, drill, retain.

A typical session takes 10-15 minutes. The system adapts to your available time. Even a short session is useful because the algorithm selects the highest-value drills for right now. Fifteen minutes a day, five days a week, is enough to make measurable progress.

Learning and Features

HSK 1-6 with full content. HSK 7-9 is in development. The free tier covers HSK 1-2. Full Access covers everything through HSK 6. The curriculum is aligned to the official HSK word lists and test structure.

Mandarin uses a modified FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler) algorithm -- the same family used by Anki. On top of that, it applies Bayesian confidence dampening, which means the system needs multiple data points before trusting your mastery of a word. A few lucky guesses do not inflate your score. Review intervals are calculated from your actual recall data, not a fixed schedule.

They span six skill categories: recognition, production, listening discrimination, tone accuracy, reading comprehension, and sentence-level use. Examples include tone pair discrimination, cloze deletion, audio-to-hanzi matching, sentence construction, register-aware practice, and character-to-pinyin recall. You never see all 27 at once -- the system picks the right type for each word based on your level and which cognitive skill needs work. Most learners encounter 5-8 types per session.

Yes. Speaking drills have you record yourself saying a word or phrase, and the system grades your tone accuracy against the target. It tells you whether your tones are right -- no guessing. For free-form conversation practice with a human, you will still need a tutor or language partner. Mandarin builds the pronunciation and tone foundation that makes those conversations productive.

Yes. Your learning data is stored locally and the app works without an internet connection. You need a connection to load new graded reading passages, but your existing drill queue and study history are always available offline.

AI was used to help build the content. But when you are studying, every scheduling decision, every score, every recommendation is deterministic -- computed by explicit algorithms, not generated by an AI model. This means no hallucinated pinyin, no inconsistent grading, no API dependency, and no cost per query. The same right answer every time. The app works offline because it does not need to call any external service.

Yes. Twenty-six grammar points are integrated into drills and reading passages. Grammar is not taught as isolated rules -- it is practiced through cloze deletions, sentence construction, and contextual reading. You learn patterns by using them, not by memorizing explanations.

Mandarin does not currently include handwriting or character tracing. The focus is on recognition, production, listening, and speaking. For handwriting practice, Skritter or pen-and-paper work well alongside Mandarin. This may be added in the future, but only if it can be done well.
