DMCA Takedown Procedure

Effective: March 13, 2026

Aelu ("we," "us") respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects our users to do the same. This page describes how we handle claims of copyright infringement under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 512).

1. About Our Content

Before filing a notice, it may help to understand how content in Aelu is produced:

  • AI-generated educational content: Drill sentences, example passages, context notes, and error explanations are generated by open-source language models (Qwen 2.5) running locally on our infrastructure. All generated content passes through automated validation and human review. No content is copied from external websites, textbooks, or copyrighted corpora.
  • Dictionary data: Pinyin readings and English definitions are derived from CC-CEDICT, a collaboratively maintained Chinese-English dictionary released under the Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 3.0 License (CC BY-SA 3.0).
  • User data: Aelu stores study progress, drill history, and learning analytics. Users do not upload, post, or share content through the service.

Because Aelu does not host user-uploaded content, most copyright concerns will relate to our own generated material rather than third-party submissions. We still maintain the full DMCA procedure described below.

2. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you believe that content available through Aelu infringes your copyright, you may submit a written takedown notice to our designated DMCA agent. Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3), your notice must include all of the following:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or a person authorized to act on their behalf.
  • Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed. If your notice covers multiple works, you may provide a representative list.
  • Identification of the material you claim is infringing, with enough detail for us to locate it (e.g., a URL or specific description of the content within the app).
  • Your contact information: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf.

Send your notice to our designated DMCA agent at the address listed in Section 6 below. Incomplete notices may not receive a response. We will acknowledge receipt of properly formed notices within 5 business days.

3. Counter-Notification Procedure

If content you are associated with has been removed or disabled in response to a DMCA takedown notice, and you believe the removal was the result of a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification. Your counter-notification must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature.
  • Identification of the material that was removed or disabled, and the location where it appeared before removal.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  • Your name, address, and telephone number.
  • A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the United States, any judicial district in which Aelu may be found), and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original takedown notice or their agent.

Send your counter-notification to our designated DMCA agent at the address listed in Section 6 below.

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification, we will forward it to the party who filed the original takedown notice. If the original complainant does not file a court action seeking to restrain the allegedly infringing activity within 10 business days of receiving the counter-notification, we will restore the removed material within 10 to 14 business days after receiving the counter-notification.

4. Repeat Infringer Policy

Aelu maintains a policy for terminating the accounts of users who are repeat infringers of copyright, in appropriate circumstances. Specifically:

  • We track DMCA takedown notices associated with user accounts, where applicable.
  • Users who receive three valid DMCA takedown notices may have their accounts terminated.
  • We consider the circumstances of each case. A counter-notification that is not challenged resets the count for that notice.
  • Account termination under this policy is at our sole discretion and will be communicated to the account holder.

5. Good Faith Requirements

Filing a DMCA takedown notice is a serious legal action. Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing may be liable for damages, including costs and attorney's fees. Before filing a notice, please consider:

  • Whether the use qualifies as fair use under 17 U.S.C. § 107.
  • Whether you actually own or are authorized to enforce the copyright in question.
  • Whether the content was independently created (as is the case with most of our AI-generated material).

We take every notice seriously, but we also take the rights of content creators and our users seriously. We will not remove content in response to notices that are clearly frivolous, abusive, or filed in bad faith.

For general inquiries not related to copyright, email hello@aeluapp.com.

7. Modifications

We may update this DMCA procedure from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with a revised effective date. The current version always applies to any notice received after its effective date.
