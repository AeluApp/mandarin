Accessibility Statement

Last updated: March 2026

Aelu is committed to making Chinese language learning accessible to as many people as possible. We work toward conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA.

What We Currently Support

  • Keyboard navigation: All core learning flows (drills, reading, settings) are accessible via keyboard. Skip-to-content link is provided.
  • Screen reader compatibility: Semantic HTML, ARIA roles, and live regions for dynamic content updates.
  • Color contrast: Text meets WCAG AA contrast ratios in both light and dark modes.
  • Dark mode: Automatic dark theme based on system preference (prefers-color-scheme), reducing eye strain.
  • Text scaling: Interface scales correctly up to 200% zoom without loss of content or functionality.
  • No flashing content: No animations that flash more than three times per second.
  • Audio alternatives: Drill content is always presented in text (hanzi, pinyin, English) alongside audio. Audio is supplementary, not required.

Known Limitations

  • Speaking drills: The tone grading feature requires voice input, which may not be accessible to all users. Speaking drills can be skipped without penalty to progress.
  • Chinese character rendering: Some screen readers may not correctly announce Chinese characters or pinyin tone marks. We provide English translations alongside all Chinese text.
  • Complex data visualizations: Diagnostic charts and progress graphs may not be fully accessible to screen readers. We provide text summaries of key data points.

Our Commitment

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We regularly test with keyboard-only navigation and screen reader software (VoiceOver on macOS/iOS). When we find issues, we prioritize fixes.

We have not yet completed a formal third-party accessibility audit. As the product matures, we plan to engage an accessibility specialist for a comprehensive WCAG 2.1 AA audit.

Feedback

If you encounter an accessibility barrier while using Aelu, please let us know. We take these reports seriously and will work to address them.

Email: hello@aeluapp.com

Please include a description of the issue, what assistive technology you are using (if applicable), and the page or feature where you encountered the problem.
