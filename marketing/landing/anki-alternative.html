What you get instead of deck-building

  • A ready-to-go HSK curriculum — 10,000+ items across HSK 1-9. No shared decks of questionable quality.
  • 44 drill types, not just front/back cards — tone pairs, cloze deletion, listening comprehension, character recognition, sentence construction, and more.
  • The cleanup loop — read Chinese passages, tap words you don't know, and those exact words appear in your next drill session. Anki can't do this.
  • Adaptive scheduling — the system tracks which skills are weakest (listening? tones? reading?) and adjusts your session mix automatically.
  • Context notes — every word has handwritten usage notes, register information, and common collocations. Not auto-generated.
  • Diagnostics — HSK readiness projections, weak spot analysis, and progress tracking that goes beyond "cards reviewed."

Honest take

If you love building decks, tweaking card templates, and having total control over your SRS — Anki is still great. It's free, it's powerful, and it's battle-tested.

Aelu is for the learner who wants to study Chinese, not build a study system. You open the app, start a session, and the system handles the rest.
