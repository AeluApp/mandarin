# App Store Preview Video Storyboard

**Duration:** 25 seconds
**Resolution:** 1080x1920 (vertical, iPhone)
**Frame rate:** 60fps
**Audio:** Instrumental only, no voiceover

---

## Audio Direction

Calm, warm instrumental. Solo piano or piano with light strings. Tempo around 70-80 BPM. Think Uematsu at his most restrained — the Fisherman's Horizon melody energy, not the battle theme energy. The music should feel like a quiet room with good light.

No sound effects for UI interactions. The only non-music audio is a single soft chime at the very end (the session-complete sound from the app).

---

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: Opening (0:00 - 0:04)

**Duration:** 4 seconds

**Visual:** Warm stone background (#F2EBE0) fills the screen. The 漫 character fades in at center, large, in dusty rose (#946070). It holds for a beat, then gently drifts upward as the app interface fades in beneath it.

**Text overlay:** None. Let the character speak.

**Transition:** Crossfade into Scene 2.

**Notes:** The upward drift matches the "upward-drift motion" design language. Fade-in should take about 1.5 seconds. The character should feel like it is surfacing from the background, not flying in.

---

### Scene 2: Dashboard (0:04 - 0:08)

**Duration:** 4 seconds

**Visual:** The main dashboard, fully loaded with realistic mid-journey data. Camera holds steady — no unnecessary panning or zooming. The interface is simply presented, warm and readable. A subtle pulse on the "Begin Session" area draws the eye.

**Text overlay:** "Study Chinese honestly." appears above the device frame in Cormorant Garamond, dusty rose.

**Transition:** The user taps "Begin Session." The screen transitions naturally into the drill view.

**Notes:** Show real data — items due, mastery indicators, session history. The dashboard should communicate "this is a tool that respects your intelligence."

---

### Scene 3: Drill Sequence (0:08 - 0:14)

**Duration:** 6 seconds

**Visual:** Quick montage of three drill types, each shown for about 2 seconds:

1. **Tone identification** (2s): A hanzi appears. Four tone options shown. The correct answer is tapped. A calm correct indicator appears (no confetti, no explosions — just a clean confirmation).

2. **Character recognition** (2s): A pinyin prompt appears. Four hanzi options. Correct selection.

3. **Context matching** (2s): A sentence with a blank. The user selects the right word from options. Clean confirmation.

**Text overlay:** "Twelve drill types." appears during the transition between drill 2 and drill 3.

**Transition:** Smooth slide to reading view.

**Notes:** The drill interactions should feel natural and quick. Tap animations should be subtle. The point is to show variety and cleanliness, not to demonstrate every detail.

---

### Scene 4: Graded Reading (0:14 - 0:18)

**Duration:** 4 seconds

**Visual:** A graded reading passage fills the screen. Chinese text is clearly legible. The user taps a word — a clean popover shows pinyin, definition, and HSK level. The popover dismisses and the user scrolls slightly, showing more of the passage.

**Text overlay:** "Read at your level." appears above the device frame.

**Transition:** Crossfade to progress view.

**Notes:** The reading passage should be real Chinese at about HSK 2-3. The word lookup interaction should feel effortless. Show that this is a reading tool, not just a flashcard app.

---

### Scene 5: Progress Tracking (0:18 - 0:22)

**Duration:** 4 seconds

**Visual:** The progress view with clear data visualization. Show: a mastery curve trending upward over time, HSK level projections with multi-criteria breakdown, specific areas of strength and weakness identified. The data looks honest — not a perfect curve, real variation.

**Text overlay:** "See what you actually know." in dusty rose above the frame.

**Transition:** The screen dims slightly as dark mode activates — a smooth, warm transition.

**Notes:** The progress data is the credibility moment. It needs to look real and useful, not like a vanity dashboard. Charts should use the brand palette.

---

### Scene 6: Dark Mode (0:22 - 0:24)

**Duration:** 2 seconds

**Visual:** The same progress view, now in warm dark mode. The transition should feel like the room dimming to a comfortable evening glow. Colors adapt — warm backgrounds, readable text, accent colors still present.

**Text overlay:** None needed. The visual transition speaks for itself.

**Transition:** Quick fade to closing card.

**Notes:** This is a brief moment — just enough to show that dark mode exists and is warm, not harsh.

---

### Scene 7: Closing (0:24 - 0:25)

**Duration:** 1 second (holds until video ends)

**Visual:** Warm stone background. "Mandarin" in large Cormorant Garamond, warm navy (#2A3650). "Patient Chinese Study" below in Source Sans 3, lighter weight. The 漫 character sits quietly in the bottom right, small, in dusty rose.

**Text overlay:** Integrated into the closing card.

**Audio:** The session-complete chime from the app plays softly as the closing card appears. Music fades.

**Transition:** Hold until end.

**Notes:** No call to action text like "Download Now!" The closing card is the brand itself. The App Store provides the download button.

---

## Production Notes

- **Total duration:** 25 seconds (within 15-30 second requirement)
- **No voiceover.** The app speaks through its design.
- **All interactions should use natural timing.** Do not speed up taps or transitions to cram in more content. If something needs to be cut, cut a scene rather than rushing.
- **Screen recordings should be from a real running app** with realistic data, not mockups. This is a trust-building video.
- **The video should feel quiet.** Most App Store previews are loud and fast. This one is deliberately slow and warm. That is the differentiation.
- **Captions:** Apple requires that the video work without sound. The text overlays serve this purpose. Verify legibility with audio muted.
- **File format:** H.264, .mp4 container
- **Also produce:** iPad version (landscape, same scenes, adapted layouts) and a 6.5" version (identical to 6.7" with dimension adjustment)
