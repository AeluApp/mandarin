# Messaging Guide — Aelu

This is the operational playbook for anyone writing copy for Aelu: landing pages, ads, emails, social media, partner communications. Every claim must be defensible. Every metric must be real. Every sentence should respect the reader's intelligence.

**Brand voice:** Calm, adult, data-grounded, no praise inflation.
**Aesthetic:** "Civic Sanctuary" — warm stone + teal + terracotta.
**Philosophy:** Honest metrics, respect for the learner's time and intelligence.
**Anti-patterns:** No cartoon mascots, no "Great job!", no artificial urgency, no dark patterns, no emoji-heavy copy.
**Current tagline:** "Adaptive Chinese learning for serious learners."

## Objection Handling

For each objection: what they say, why they think it, the honest answer, and how to say it in copy.

### 1. "That's more expensive than Duolingo."

**Why they think this:** Duolingo Super is $7/month. Aelu is $14.99/month. The math is simple.

**The honest answer:** Duolingo has ~3 drill formats and no graded reading. Aelu has 44 drill types, graded reading with a cleanup loop, adaptive listening, speaking drills, and HSK diagnostics. They serve different stages of learning. If Duolingo is working for you, keep using it.

**Copy version:** "Duolingo is great for getting started. Aelu is for what comes after. 44 drill types, graded reading, listening practice, and diagnostics for $8 more per month."

---

### 2. "Anki is free."

**Why they think this:** Anki is genuinely free (desktop). It's powerful. They're already invested in their workflow.

**The honest answer:** Anki is an incredible tool. But it's a blank SRS engine — you supply the content, the curriculum, and the drill logic. Aelu gives you all of that pre-built: 44 drill types, graded reading that feeds your queue, HSK-aligned content, and multi-skill tracking. If you love building decks, Anki is perfect. If you want to just study, try this.

**Copy version:** "Anki is free and powerful. Aelu is $14.99/month and you never build a deck. Different tools for different preferences."

---

### 3. "I can find free resources online."

**Why they think this:** There are free HSK word lists, YouTube channels, graded readers, and community Anki decks everywhere.

**The honest answer:** You absolutely can. The question is whether you want to spend time assembling resources or spend time studying. Aelu integrates SRS, graded reading, listening, speaking, and diagnostics in one place. The free tier lets you try all of it without paying.

**Copy version:** "Free resources are everywhere. A system that connects them — reading to drills, drills to diagnostics, diagnostics to your next session — is what you're paying for."

---

### 4. "HelloChinese is cheaper."

**Why they think this:** HelloChinese premium is ~$10/month. Comparable feature set on the surface.

**The honest answer:** HelloChinese is a solid app. It has more polished onboarding and better character writing practice. Aelu has more drill variety (27 vs ~6), the cleanup loop, Bayesian scheduling, and multi-skill diagnostics. Try both free tiers and see which approach fits how you learn.

**Copy version:** "HelloChinese is good. We think we're better at adaptive drilling and reading-to-review integration. But try both — the free tiers are real."

---

### 5. "I need a real teacher, not an app."

**Why they think this:** Teachers provide feedback, conversation practice, and cultural context that no app can replicate.

**The honest answer:** They're right. A teacher is better for speaking practice, cultural nuance, and real-time correction. Aelu doesn't replace a teacher — it handles the parts a teacher shouldn't have to: vocabulary drilling, review scheduling, tone discrimination practice, and reading exposure. Use both.

**Copy version:** "A teacher teaches. Aelu drills. Use your teacher's time for conversation and culture — let the system handle review scheduling and gap identification."

---

### 6. "I tried SRS before and quit."

**Why they think this:** Most SRS experiences are Anki — a blank engine that requires setup, deck management, and discipline with no variety.

**The honest answer:** SRS with one drill type (flashcards) gets monotonous. That's a real problem. Aelu uses 44 drill types with interleaving — your brain doesn't get bored because the next question is always a different format. Sessions are 15 minutes, not open-ended. The cleanup loop means you're always drilling words you recently encountered, not stale cards from months ago.

**Copy version:** "SRS with one flashcard format is boring. SRS with 44 drill types, interleaving, and a 15-minute cap is a different experience."

---

### 7. "44 drill types sounds overwhelming."

**Why they think this:** 27 is a big number. They imagine having to learn 27 different interfaces.

**The honest answer:** You never see all 27 at once. The system selects the right drill type for each word based on your level and which cognitive skill needs practice. You just answer questions — the system handles which type to serve. Most learners encounter 5-8 types per session.

**Copy version:** "You don't pick from 27 options. The system picks for you. You just answer the question in front of you."

## Message Testing Framework

### A/B Test Priorities (Ranked)

Test the messages that affect the highest-leverage decisions first.

**Priority 1: Landing page headline**
Why: The headline determines whether someone reads further. Small changes yield disproportionate impact on conversion. Test the primary value proposition framing.
- Test A: Feature-led ("44 drill types. Graded reading. Adaptive scheduling.")
- Test B: Outcome-led ("Learn Chinese in 15 minutes a day. Actually retain it.")
- Test C: Problem-led ("Your Chinese app drills random vocabulary. This one drills your real gaps.")

**Priority 2: Free tier CTA wording**
Why: The free tier is the primary conversion mechanism. Test whether people respond more to low-commitment framing or specific-value framing.
- Test A: "Start free" (minimal commitment language)
- Test B: "Try your first session" (specific and tangible)
- Test C: "See where you stand" (curiosity-driven)

**Priority 3: Primary value proposition**
Why: This appears everywhere — ads, social, email, partner materials. Getting it right has compounding returns.
- Test A: Cleanup loop focused ("Every word you look up becomes practice")
- Test B: Anti-gamification focused ("No streaks. No hearts. Just learning.")
- Test C: Breadth focused ("44 drill types, graded reading, and diagnostics in one place")

**Priority 4: Upgrade prompt messaging**
Why: Free-to-paid conversion is the revenue bottleneck. Test what motivates the upgrade.
- Test A: Content-focused ("HSK 3-6: 4,700 additional words")
- Test B: Feature-focused ("Unlock speaking drills, advanced diagnostics, and all 44 drill types")
- Test C: Progress-focused ("Your HSK 1-2 foundation is solid. Here's the next step.")

**Priority 5: Ad creative angle**
Why: Determines cost per acquisition. Test which story resonates in paid channels.
- Test A: Builder story ("I'm learning Mandarin and built my own app")
- Test B: Cleanup loop demo ("Watch what happens when you look up a word")
- Test C: Anti-Duolingo ("I used Duolingo for 2 years and couldn't read a menu")

**Priority 6: Email subject lines**
Why: Open rates determine whether any email content matters. Test curiosity vs. clarity.
- Test A: Specific ("Your listening is lagging behind your vocabulary")
- Test B: Provocative ("Streaks are lying to you")
- Test C: Direct ("New: listening speed control")

**Priority 7: Social media hook**
Why: First line determines whether someone reads the rest. Test opening strategies.
- Test A: Data hook ("60% of new vocabulary is forgotten within 48 hours")
- Test B: Contrarian hook ("Streaks are bad for language learning")
- Test C: Personal hook ("I've been learning Mandarin for [X] months. Here's what actually works.")

---

### Test Structure

**How to set up landing page message tests:**

1. **One variable at a time.** Change the headline OR the CTA OR the subhead. Never change two things simultaneously — you won't know which caused the difference.

2. **Minimum sample size.** Run each variant until you have at least 200 unique visitors per variant. Below that, the data is noise.

3. **Duration.** Run tests for at least 7 full days to account for day-of-week variation. Monday visitors behave differently from Sunday visitors.

4. **Traffic split.** 50/50 for two variants. 33/33/33 for three. Use URL parameters or a simple A/B tool (Google Optimize successor, Posthog, or even manual rotation).

5. **What to measure.** Define the success metric before starting. Don't change it mid-test.

6. **When to call it.** Use a significance calculator (e.g., Evan Miller's). Stop when you reach 95% confidence or when you've run for 14 days — whichever comes first. If no significance after 14 days, the difference doesn't matter. Pick whichever you prefer and move on.

7. **Document everything.** Log the test, the variants, the dates, the sample sizes, and the result. Past tests inform future tests.

## Quick Reference: What Not to Say

These phrases and patterns are off-limits. They contradict the brand voice.

| Never say | Why | Say instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "Great job!" | Praise inflation. The system doesn't know if it was great. | "Session complete. 78% accuracy." |
| "Keep your streak alive!" | Streak anxiety is a dark pattern. | (Don't mention streaks at all.) |
| "You're falling behind!" | Guilt-based motivation. Disrespectful. | "It's been 9 days. Expect some rust." |
| "Unlock your potential!" | Vague, corporate, means nothing. | "Here's what the data says about your progress." |
| "Join thousands of learners!" | We don't have thousands yet. Even if we did, social proof by headcount is weak. | "Free to try. Judge it yourself." |
| "Limited time offer!" | Artificial urgency is a dark pattern. | "$14.99/month. Same price always." |
| "You won't believe..." | Clickbait. Disrespectful of the reader. | (Just state the fact.) |
| "Fluent in 3 months!" | Irresponsible. Chinese takes years. | "Measurable progress in weeks. Fluency takes years. We're honest about that." |
| "AI-powered learning!" | The system is explicitly not AI-powered at runtime. | "Deterministic scheduling. No AI at runtime." |
| "The best app for..." | Unprovable superlative. | "An app built for [specific audience]." |
| "Fun and easy!" | Chinese isn't easy. And "fun" undersells the work. | "Effective and sustainable." |
| "Gamify your learning!" | The entire brand is anti-gamification. | "Study, don't play." |
