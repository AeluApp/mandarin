### Short 9: The 3rd Tone Myth
**Hook:** "You're probably saying the 3rd tone wrong."
**Script:**
[Text on screen: mǎ — the classic "dipping" tone]
Most textbooks teach the 3rd tone as a full dip: down then up.
In natural speech? It's almost never that.
[Text: show pitch contour — low, flat]
In most contexts, the 3rd tone is just LOW. A low flat tone that doesn't rise.
The full dip only happens in isolation or very slow speech.
[Text: show 你好 nǐhǎo → tone sandhi]
And when two 3rd tones meet? The first one becomes a 2nd tone. 你好 is actually pronounced "níhǎo."
If your tones sound "textbook-y," this might be why.

### Short 12: Measure Words in 60 Seconds
**Hook:** "Chinese has a grammar concept English doesn't: measure words."
**Script:**
In English: three books. Simple.
In Chinese: 三本书. Three [bound-object-classifier] books.
[Text: show examples]
一杯水 — one [cup] water (a glass of water)
两只猫 — two [small-animal] cats
三个人 — three [general] people
Every noun has a specific measure word. 本 for books, 杯 for cups, 只 for small animals, 个 for general.
[Text: show 个]
Good news: 个 works as a default for most things when you're starting out. Technically wrong sometimes, but always understood.
Learn the common ones. Default to 个 for the rest.

**Hook:** "You forget 60% of new vocabulary within 48 hours."

[Text: show SRS review points on the curve]
